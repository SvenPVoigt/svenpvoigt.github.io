title: What does the reverse repo reaching $1.7T mean?
description: High level views and discussion of the reverse repo reaching $1.7T
---

There's so much money in the system that every possible yielding asset has been bid up to the point that it barely returns any yield. The RRP being at 1.7 trillion is evidence of this, as banks and MMs cannot find any assets they believe to return a positive yield.

This can unwind through inflation, where asset values again make sense relative to the new lower value of the dollar, or deflation, where velocity of money essentially grinds to a halt because no assets can return a yield. Deflation would be an uncontrolled positive feedback loop where asset values make even less sense and velocity further falls to 0 (as happened in Japan in the 1980s).

The Fed is aware of this. They know the only successful way out is high inflation. But the tools they have are yielding less and less success - as seen in the RRP - their money just comes right back to them since assets yield nothing. In a way though they are being saved by the supply issues actually causing inflation. I doubt they will raise rates since it would be a potential deflation nightmare. I could see them choosing negative rates to reduce deflationary pressures from other countries investing in the dollar though.
